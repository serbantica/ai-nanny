# Health Kinetics and Movement Monitoring Procedures

## Overview

Health kinetics encompasses the monitoring, assessment, and optimization of physical movement, vital signs, and overall physical health status of residents. This document outlines procedures for AI companion systems and caregivers to track, analyze, and respond to kinetic health indicators.

## Vital Signs Monitoring

### Standard Monitoring Schedule

#### Daily Measurements (Morning - 7:00 AM to 9:00 AM)
- **Blood Pressure**: Systolic/Diastolic reading
- **Heart Rate**: Beats per minute (BPM)
- **Temperature**: Oral, tympanic, or temporal
- **Respiratory Rate**: Breaths per minute
- **Oxygen Saturation (SpO2)**: Pulse oximetry reading

#### Weekly Measurements (Monday Morning)
- **Weight**: Standing or chair scale
- **Body Mass Index (BMI)**: Calculate and track
- **Grip Strength**: Both hands, measured in kg
- **Standing Balance**: Timed single-leg stand

#### Monthly Assessments (First Monday of Month)
- **Gait Analysis**: Walking speed and pattern
- **Range of Motion**: Major joints assessment
- **Flexibility Tests**: Sit-and-reach, shoulder mobility
- **Endurance Test**: 6-minute walk test

### Normal Range Guidelines

| Vital Sign | Normal Range | Concern Level | Action Required |
|------------|--------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Blood Pressure | 90/60 - 120/80 mmHg | >140/90 or <90/60 | Notify nurse immediately |
| Heart Rate | 60-100 BPM | <50 or >110 BPM | Monitor closely, alert if sustained |
| Temperature | 97.8°F - 99.1°F | >100.4°F or <95°F | Contact medical staff |
| Respiratory Rate | 12-20 breaths/min | <10 or >25 | Emergency alert |
| SpO2 | >95% | <90% | Immediate medical attention |
| Weight Change | ±2 lbs/week | >5 lbs/week | Nutritionist consultation |

## Movement Tracking and Analysis

### Daily Activity Monitoring

#### Movement Metrics to Track
1. **Step Count**: Target 2,000-5,000 steps per day (adjusted per resident)
2. **Active Minutes**: Time spent in light to moderate activity
3. **Sedentary Time**: Monitor prolonged sitting (>2 hours continuous)
4. **Sleep Movement**: Nighttime activity and sleep quality
5. **Transfer Count**: Number of sit-to-stand transitions

#### Activity Zones
- **Bedroom**: Sleep patterns, nighttime mobility
- **Bathroom**: Frequency, duration, nighttime visits
- **Common Areas**: Social engagement, group activities
- **Outdoor Areas**: Garden walks, fresh air exposure
- **Dining Room**: Meal times, eating duration

### Gait and Mobility Assessment

#### Gait Parameters
- **Walking Speed**: Normal >0.8 m/s, Concern <0.6 m/s
- **Stride Length**: Measure distance between steps
- **Step Width**: Assess stability (normal 5-10 cm)
- **Double Support Time**: Both feet on ground percentage
- **Cadence**: Steps per minute (normal 90-120)

#### Mobility Levels
1. **Independent**: No assistance required
2. **Supervision Only**: Nearby assistance for safety
3. **Minimal Assistance**: Hand-hold or light support
4. **Moderate Assistance**: Significant support needed
5. **Maximum Assistance**: Two-person assist or mechanical lift

### Fall Risk Assessment

#### Daily Fall Risk Indicators
- Recent falls or near-falls in past 30 days
- Unsteady gait or balance issues
- Use of mobility aids (cane, walker, wheelchair)
- Medications affecting balance or cognition
- Environmental hazards in living space
- Vision or hearing impairments
- Orthostatic hypotension (dizziness when standing)

#### Fall Prevention Strategies
1. **Environmental Modifications**
   - Remove tripping hazards (cords, rugs)
   - Install grab bars in bathrooms
   - Ensure adequate lighting (night lights)
   - Clear pathways in living spaces
   - Non-slip flooring in wet areas

2. **Assistive Devices**
   - Properly fitted mobility aids
   - Bed rails if approved by medical team
   - Wheelchair with working brakes
   - Reaching tools to avoid overextending
   - Proper footwear (non-slip, supportive)

3. **Monitoring Protocols**
   - Visual checks every 15-30 minutes for high-risk residents
   - Bed/chair alarms for wandering risk
   - Wearable fall detection devices
   - Motion sensors in room
   - Regular gait assessments

## Physical Therapy and Exercise Protocols

### Therapeutic Exercise Programs

#### Strength Training (3x per week)
**Upper Body:**
- Seated arm raises with light weights (1-3 lbs)
- Bicep curls with resistance bands
- Shoulder rotations and stretches
- Grip strengthening exercises
- Wall push-ups or modified push-ups

**Lower Body:**
- Seated or standing leg lifts
- Heel and toe raises
- Mini squats or chair stands
- Hip abduction exercises
- Ankle circles and flexion

**Core Stability:**
- Seated torso rotations
- Abdominal bracing exercises
- Pelvic tilts
- Modified planks (wall or table)
- Balance board exercises

#### Flexibility and Stretching (Daily)
**Morning Routine (10 minutes):**
1. Neck rolls and stretches
2. Shoulder circles and arm crosses
3. Trunk rotations
4. Hip flexor stretches
5. Hamstring stretches (seated)
6. Calf stretches
7. Ankle rotations
8. Deep breathing exercises

**Evening Routine (5 minutes):**
1. Gentle neck stretches
2. Shoulder shrugs and releases
3. Wrist and hand stretches
4. Seated spinal twists
5. Deep breathing and relaxation

#### Balance Training (Daily)
**Progression Levels:**
1. **Level 1**: Seated balance exercises
2. **Level 2**: Standing with support (counter, chair)
3. **Level 3**: Standing with light support (one finger)
4. **Level 4**: Standing without support
5. **Level 5**: Single-leg stands and dynamic movements

**Balance Exercises:**
- Weight shifts (side to side, forward and back)
- Marching in place
- Heel-to-toe walking
- Single-leg stands (5-30 seconds)
- Tandem stance
- Figure-8 walking patterns

### Cardiovascular Conditioning

#### Aerobic Activity Guidelines
- **Frequency**: 5 days per week minimum
- **Duration**: 20-30 minutes per session
- **Intensity**: Light to moderate (able to talk comfortably)
- **Types**: Walking, stationary cycling, chair aerobics, water exercises

#### Heart Rate Monitoring
- **Target Zone**: 50-70% of maximum heart rate
- **Maximum HR**: 220 - age (approximate)
- **Example**: 80-year-old target = 70-98 BPM during exercise
- **Recovery**: HR should return near baseline within 10 minutes

#### Exercise Safety Guidelines
1. **Pre-Exercise Check:**
   - Resting heart rate and blood pressure
   - Current medication effects
   - Recent meals (wait 30-60 minutes after eating)
   - Proper hydration status
   - Appropriate clothing and footwear

2. **During Exercise:**
   - Monitor for signs of distress
   - Check vital signs every 10 minutes for high-risk residents
   - Ensure proper form to prevent injury
   - Maintain hydration (water available)
   - Stop if chest pain, dizziness, or severe shortness of breath

3. **Post-Exercise:**
   - Cool-down period (5 minutes light activity)
   - Stretching routine
   - Vital signs check
   - Hydration
   - Rest period before next activity

## Postural Assessment and Correction

### Common Postural Issues

#### Forward Head Posture
**Assessment:**
- Head positioned forward of shoulders
- Rounded upper back
- Neck strain and headaches

**Corrections:**
- Chin tucks (10 reps, 3x daily)
- Shoulder blade squeezes
- Chest stretches
- Ergonomic seating adjustments

#### Kyphosis (Rounded Upper Back)
**Assessment:**
- Excessive thoracic spine curvature
- Difficulty standing upright
- Breathing limitations

**Corrections:**
- Upper back extension exercises
- Thoracic foam rolling
- Pectoral stretches
- Posture awareness training

#### Scoliosis or Lateral Imbalances
**Assessment:**
- Uneven shoulders or hips
- Asymmetrical stance
- One-sided muscle tension

**Corrections:**
- Symmetry-focused exercises
- Core strengthening
- Stretching tight side muscles
- Physical therapy referral if severe

### Seating and Positioning Guidelines

#### Proper Seated Posture
- **Feet**: Flat on floor or footrest
- **Knees**: At 90-degree angle, level with or slightly below hips
- **Hips**: Back against chair, pelvis neutral
- **Spine**: Upright with natural curves, lumbar support
- **Shoulders**: Relaxed, not hunched
- **Head**: Over shoulders, chin parallel to ground

#### Wheelchair Positioning
- **Seat Depth**: 2-3 inches between seat edge and back of knees
- **Seat Height**: Feet flat on footrests or floor
- **Backrest**: Supports spine, allows arm movement
- **Armrests**: Elbows at 90 degrees when relaxed
- **Cushioning**: Pressure-relief cushion if seated >2 hours

#### Bed Positioning
- **Head Elevation**: 30-45 degrees for comfort, reduce aspiration risk
- **Leg Support**: Slight knee bend with pillow if needed
- **Side-Lying**: Pillows between knees and under top arm
- **Repositioning**: Every 2 hours for bed-bound residents

## Pain and Discomfort Management

### Pain Assessment Protocol

#### Pain Evaluation (Regular Schedule)
- **Daily**: Morning check-in, ask about pain level
- **Pre-Activity**: Before exercise or therapy
- **Post-Activity**: After physical activities
- **As-Needed**: When resident reports discomfort

#### Pain Rating Scale (0-10)
- **0**: No pain
- **1-3**: Mild pain, minimal interference
- **4-6**: Moderate pain, some interference with activities
- **7-9**: Severe pain, significant impact on function
- **10**: Worst imaginable pain

#### Pain Location and Character
- **Location**: Point to or describe area
- **Type**: Sharp, dull, aching, burning, tingling
- **Duration**: Constant, intermittent, specific times
- **Triggers**: Movement, position, activity
- **Relief Factors**: Rest, medication, heat/cold, position change

### Non-Pharmacological Pain Management

#### Physical Interventions
1. **Heat Therapy**
   - Warm compresses for muscle tension
   - Heating pad (15-20 minutes, medium setting)
   - Warm bath or shower
   - Paraffin wax for arthritic hands

2. **Cold Therapy**
   - Ice packs for acute inflammation (15 minutes)
   - Cold compresses for swelling
   - Never apply directly to skin (use barrier)

3. **Massage and Manual Therapy**
   - Gentle massage for muscle tension
   - Range of motion exercises
   - Myofascial release techniques
   - Professional physical therapy when indicated

4. **Positioning and Support**
   - Proper body alignment
   - Supportive cushions and pillows
   - Pressure relief for prolonged sitting/lying
   - Elevation of swollen limbs

#### Complementary Approaches
- **Relaxation Techniques**: Deep breathing, progressive muscle relaxation
- **Distraction**: Music, games, conversation, activities
- **Guided Imagery**: Visualization exercises
- **Mindfulness**: Present-moment awareness practices
- **Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation (TENS)**: With physician approval

### When to Escalate to Medical Team

#### Immediate Notification Required
- New onset severe pain (7-10)
- Chest pain or difficulty breathing
- Sudden loss of movement or sensation
- Severe headache with neurological symptoms
- Pain with signs of infection (fever, redness, swelling)
- Fall with subsequent pain or inability to bear weight
- Pain medication not providing relief

## Rehabilitation and Recovery Protocols

### Post-Fall Recovery Protocol

#### Immediate Post-Fall (0-24 hours)
1. **Assessment**: Medical evaluation completed
2. **Monitoring**: Vital signs every 2-4 hours
3. **Pain Management**: Per physician orders
4. **Rest**: Limit activities, supervised mobility only
5. **Ice/Heat**: As appropriate for injuries

#### Early Recovery (24 hours - 1 week)
1. **Gentle Range of Motion**: 3x daily
2. **Assisted Mobility**: With supervision
3. **Pain Reassessment**: Daily evaluation
4. **Psychological Support**: Address fear of falling
5. **Environmental Review**: Identify and correct hazards

#### Progressive Rehabilitation (1 week - 4 weeks)
1. **Increasing Independence**: Gradual reduction of assistance
2. **Strength Building**: Targeted exercises for weak areas
3. **Balance Retraining**: Progressive balance program
4. **Confidence Building**: Positive reinforcement, success experiences
5. **Activity Reintegration**: Return to normal routine

### Post-Illness Reconditioning

#### After Respiratory Illness
- **Breathing Exercises**: Deep breathing, incentive spirometry
- **Gradual Activity Increase**: Start with 5-minute walks
- **Energy Conservation**: Rest between activities
- **Nutrition Support**: Adequate protein and calories
- **Monitoring**: SpO2 levels during activity

#### After Cardiac Event
- **Cardiac Rehab Protocol**: Follow physician guidelines
- **Monitored Exercise**: Heart rate and blood pressure checks
- **Progressive Intensity**: Slow increases over weeks
- **Medication Compliance**: Critical for recovery
- **Stress Management**: Relaxation techniques

#### After Surgery or Hospitalization
- **Wound Care**: Per post-operative instructions
- **Pain Control**: Scheduled and as-needed medications
- **Mobility Progression**: Bed → Chair → Walking
- **Prevent Complications**: Deep breathing, leg exercises, position changes
- **Nutritional Support**: Healing requires adequate nutrition

## Technology Integration

### Wearable Health Monitors

#### Recommended Devices
- **Fitness Trackers**: Step count, heart rate, sleep tracking
- **Fall Detection Watches**: Automatic alert systems
- **Blood Pressure Monitors**: Bluetooth-enabled, automatic logging
- **Continuous Glucose Monitors**: For diabetic residents
- **Pulse Oximeters**: Clip-on or wearable SpO2 monitors

#### Data Collection and Analysis
- **Real-Time Monitoring**: Alert for abnormal readings
- **Trend Analysis**: Identify patterns over days/weeks
- **Automated Reports**: Daily, weekly, monthly summaries
- **Predictive Analytics**: Early warning for health changes
- **Integration**: Sync with electronic health records

### AI Companion Health Monitoring

#### Automated Checks
- **Movement Sensors**: Track room-to-room activity
- **Bathroom Monitoring**: Frequency and duration patterns
- **Sleep Analysis**: Quality and duration tracking
- **Voice Analysis**: Changes in speech patterns
- **Facial Recognition**: Detect pain or distress expressions

#### Alert Thresholds
- **Prolonged Inactivity**: No movement >2 hours during day
- **Bathroom Changes**: Significant increase or decrease in visits
- **Sleep Disruption**: Multiple awakenings, total <6 hours
- **Gait Changes**: Slower speed, unsteady pattern
- **Vital Sign Abnormalities**: Outside normal ranges

## Documentation Requirements

### Daily Health Kinetics Log

**Required Documentation:**
- Date and time of observations
- Vital signs readings
- Activity levels and step counts
- Pain reports and interventions
- Exercise participation and tolerance
- Notable mobility changes
- Fall or near-fall incidents
- Equipment use (walker, cane, etc.)
- Resident comments about health status

### Weekly Summary Report

**Include:**
- Average vital signs for week
- Total weekly activity metrics
- Exercise program compliance
- Progress toward health goals
- Notable improvements or concerns
- Any new symptoms or complaints
- Changes in mobility or independence level
- Caregiver observations and recommendations

### Monthly Comprehensive Assessment

**Complete Evaluation:**
- Full physical assessment results
- Comparison to previous month data
- Progress on rehabilitation goals
- Equipment needs and modifications
- Care plan updates
- Family communication summary
- Physician consultation notes
- Plans for upcoming month

## Quality Assurance

### Monitoring Standards
- All vital signs documented within 1 hour of collection
- Movement data synced and reviewed daily
- Pain assessments completed per schedule
- Exercise logs updated after each session
- Incident reports filed within 2 hours of occurrence

### Performance Metrics
- **Compliance Rate**: >95% completion of scheduled assessments
- **Response Time**: <5 minutes for urgent alerts
- **Accuracy**: <2% error rate in vital sign documentation
- **Fall Rate**: Monitor and trend, goal <5 falls per 1000 resident days
- **Resident Satisfaction**: >90% positive feedback on health monitoring

### Staff Training Requirements
- **Initial**: 8-hour health kinetics training course
- **Annual**: 4-hour refresher and updates
- **Competency Checks**: Quarterly vital sign assessment validation
- **Emergency Response**: CPR and first aid certification maintained
- **Technology**: Training on all monitoring devices and systems

## Emergency Response Integration

### Kinetic Health Emergencies

#### Fall Response Protocol
1. Do not move resident (unless immediate danger)
2. Call for help, activate emergency system
3. Assess consciousness and breathing
4. Check for visible injuries
5. Keep resident calm and comfortable
6. Document fall circumstances
7. Complete post-fall assessment when safe

#### Sudden Mobility Loss
1. Immediate medical evaluation
2. Vital signs assessment
3. Neurological checks (stroke protocol)
4. Document onset and symptoms
5. Maintain safety during assessment

#### Severe Pain Episodes
1. Assess pain level and location
2. Check vital signs
3. Administer prescribed pain medication if available
4. Use comfort measures
5. Contact medical provider
6. Monitor response to interventions
