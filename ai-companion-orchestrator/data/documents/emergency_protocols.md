# Emergency Response Protocols for AI Companion Caregivers

## Fall Detection and Response

### Immediate Actions
When a fall is detected:
1. Immediately alert emergency services if the resident is unresponsive
2. Contact the nurse station within 30 seconds
3. Activate the fall detection alarm
4. Document the incident with timestamp and location

### Assessment Protocol
- Check for consciousness and responsiveness
- Look for visible injuries or bleeding
- Monitor vital signs if possible
- Do NOT move the resident unless there is immediate danger

### Communication Guidelines
- Speak calmly and reassuringly to the resident
- Provide comfort while waiting for medical personnel
- Notify family members within 15 minutes
- Document all communications

## Medication Management

### Medication Reminder Schedule
- Morning medications: 8:00 AM
- Afternoon medications: 2:00 PM
- Evening medications: 6:00 PM
- Bedtime medications: 9:00 PM

### Medication Administration Rules
1. Always verify resident identity using two identifiers
2. Check medication label three times
3. Ensure resident takes medication and doesn't pocket it
4. Document immediately after administration
5. Never leave medications unattended

### Missed Dose Protocol
If a medication dose is missed:
- Document the missed dose immediately
- Contact healthcare provider for instructions
- Do NOT double up on next dose without authorization
- Monitor resident for any changes

## Safety Protocols

### Wandering Prevention
- Keep exit doors alarmed
- Use GPS tracking for at-risk residents
- Maintain visual contact every 15 minutes
- Ensure proper lighting in hallways

### Infection Control
- Wash hands before and after resident contact
- Use PPE when handling bodily fluids
- Isolate residents with contagious conditions
- Clean and disinfect shared equipment

### Fire Safety
- Know location of all fire exits
- Ensure resident mobility aids are accessible
- Practice evacuation drills monthly
- Never use elevators during fire alarm

## Daily Care Activities

### Morning Routine
1. Wake resident gently at scheduled time
2. Assist with personal hygiene
3. Help with dressing
4. Provide breakfast
5. Administer morning medications
6. Engage in morning activities

### Evening Routine
1. Prepare for bedtime at scheduled time
2. Assist with changing into sleepwear
3. Ensure bathroom visit
4. Administer evening medications
5. Provide calming activities
6. Check room safety (night lights, call button)

## Communication Best Practices

### Effective Communication Techniques
- Maintain eye contact
- Speak clearly and at moderate pace
- Use simple, direct language
- Allow time for resident to process and respond
- Validate feelings and concerns
- Use gentle touch when appropriate

### Handling Difficult Behaviors
- Remain calm and patient
- Do not argue or contradict
- Redirect attention to pleasant topics
- Identify triggers and patterns
- Use distraction techniques
- Seek backup when needed

## Documentation Requirements

### Daily Logs
- Activities of daily living (ADLs)
- Meal intake and hydration
- Behavioral observations
- Sleep patterns
- Social interactions
- Mood changes

### Incident Reports
Required for:
- Falls or near-falls
- Medication errors
- Behavioral incidents
- Medical emergencies
- Equipment malfunctions
- Visitor concerns

### Report Timing

- Non-emergency incidents: Within 2 hours
