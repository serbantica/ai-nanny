# Daily Activities and Engagement Guidelines

## Introduction
Meaningful daily activities are essential for residents' physical health, mental well-being, and quality of life. This guide helps AI companions facilitate engaging, appropriate, and beneficial activities.

## Daily Activity Schedule

### Morning Routine (7:00 AM - 11:00 AM)
- **7:00-8:00 AM**: Wake-up, personal care, breakfast
- **8:30-9:30 AM**: Morning exercises or light stretching
- **9:30-10:30 AM**: Cognitive activities (puzzles, reading, games)
- **10:30-11:00 AM**: Social time (phone calls, video chats)

### Midday Activities (11:00 AM - 3:00 PM)
- **11:00-12:00 PM**: Lunch preparation and dining
- **12:00-1:00 PM**: Quiet time or rest
- **1:00-2:30 PM**: Creative activities (art, music, crafts)
- **2:30-3:00 PM**: Outdoor time (weather permitting)

### Afternoon Schedule (3:00 PM - 6:00 PM)
- **3:00-3:30 PM**: Snack and hydration break
- **3:30-4:30 PM**: Entertainment (TV, music, audiobooks)
- **4:30-5:30 PM**: Social activities or games
- **5:30-6:00 PM**: Dinner preparation

### Evening Routine (6:00 PM - 9:00 PM)
- **6:00-7:00 PM**: Dinner and family time
- **7:00-8:00 PM**: Relaxing activities (movies, reading)
- **8:00-8:30 PM**: Personal care, hygiene
- **8:30-9:00 PM**: Wind-down and bedtime preparation

## Physical Activities

### Light Exercise (Daily)
**Chair Exercises**
- Seated marching (30 seconds)
- Arm circles (10 forward, 10 backward)
- Ankle rotations (10 each direction)
- Shoulder shrugs (10 repetitions)
- Neck stretches (gentle side-to-side)

**Standing Exercises** (with support)
- Heel raises (10 repetitions)
- Sit-to-stand (5-10 repetitions)
- Side leg lifts (5 each side)
- Balance practice (hold 10 seconds)

**Walking Activities**
- Indoor walking (hallway laps)
- Outdoor strolls (weather permitting)
- Mall walking (social + exercise)
- Garden walks

### Safety Guidelines
- Always use mobility aids if prescribed
- Have stable support nearby
- Stop if dizzy or fatigued
- Wear proper footwear
- Stay hydrated
- Monitor for signs of overexertion

## Social Activities

## Entertainment and Leisure

### Television and Movies
**Recommended Content**
- Classic movies from their youth
- Nature documentaries
- Game shows
- Comedy programs
- Family-friendly series
- Historical programs

**Viewing Guidelines**
- Limit screen time to 2-3 hours per session
- Choose appropriate volume levels
- Ensure good lighting to reduce eye strain
- Take regular breaks
- Discuss content afterward

**Digital Games**

## Meal and Cooking Activities

### Kitchen Participation
**Safe Activities**
- Meal planning discussions
- Recipe selection
- Ingredient preparation (peeling, washing)
- Mixing and stirring
- Setting the table
- Taste testing

**Safety Precautions**
- No hot stove/oven operation
- Use electric appliances with supervision
- Avoid sharp knives (use safe cutters)
- Seated preparation when possible
- Good lighting and ventilation

### Nutrition Education
- Discuss healthy food choices
- Review dietary restrictions
- Share cooking memories
- Learn about new foods
- Create shopping lists

## Activity Adaptation

### For Mobility Limitations
- Seated versions of activities
- Use of adaptive equipment
- Bring activities to resident
- Adjust activity duration
- Provide proper support

### For Cognitive Impairment
- Simplify instructions
- Use visual cues
- Break into smaller steps
- Repeat as needed
- Focus on process, not outcome
- Emphasize familiar activities

### For Sensory Impairments
**Vision Loss**
- Large-print materials
- High contrast items
- Tactile activities
- Audio descriptions
- Adequate lighting

**Hearing Loss**
- Visual instructions
- Written materials
- Reduce background noise
- Face-to-face communication
- Use assistive devices

## Emergency Considerations

### Activity Safety Protocols
- Have emergency call device nearby
- Monitor for fatigue or distress
- Know resident medical conditions
- Keep first aid accessible
- Have evacuation plan
- Quick communication with staff

### Warning Signs to Monitor
- Sudden confusion or disorientation
- Excessive fatigue
- Pain or discomfort
- Difficulty breathing
- Dizziness or weakness
- Unusual behavior changes

## Quality of Life Goals

### Activity Objectives
- **Physical**: Maintain mobility and strength
- **Cognitive**: Stimulate memory and thinking
- **Social**: Foster connections and relationships
- **Emotional**: Promote happiness and purpose
- **Spiritual**: Support beliefs and practices
- **Creative**: Encourage self-expression

Remember: The best activity is one that the resident enjoys and can safely participate in. Quality over quantity, and always respect individual preferences and limitations.
