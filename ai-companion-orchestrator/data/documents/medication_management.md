# Medication Management Guide for AI Companions

## Overview
This document provides comprehensive guidelines for AI companions to assist residents with medication management, ensuring safety, compliance, and effective communication with healthcare providers.

## Daily Medication Schedule

### Morning Medications (8:00 AM)
- **Blood Pressure Medication**: Taken with breakfast
- **Thyroid Medication**: Take 30 minutes before eating
- **Vitamins and Supplements**: Take with food
- **Pain Management**: As prescribed by physician

### Afternoon Medications (2:00 PM)
- **Diabetes Medication**: Check blood sugar first
- **Heart Medication**: Monitor pulse before administration
- **Anti-inflammatory**: Take with food or milk

### Evening Medications (6:00 PM)
- **Digestive Aids**: Take 15 minutes before dinner
- **Additional Pain Management**: If needed
- **Mood Stabilizers**: As prescribed

### Bedtime Medications (9:00 PM)
- **Sleep Aids**: Only if approved by doctor
- **Blood Pressure (Evening Dose)**: If prescribed
- **Muscle Relaxants**: For chronic pain management

## Medication Reminders

### Standard Reminder Protocol
1. **15 Minutes Before**: First gentle reminder with voice notification
2. **At Scheduled Time**: Visual and audio reminder
3. **5 Minutes After**: Follow-up if medication not taken
4. **15 Minutes After**: Escalate to family member or nurse

### Reminder Communication Examples
- "Good morning! It's time for your blood pressure medication. Would you like some water?"
- "Just a friendly reminder - your afternoon medication is ready."
- "Don't forget your evening medication before dinner!"

## Safety Checks

### Pre-Administration Verification
1. **Verify Resident Identity**: Use two identifiers (name and birth date)
2. **Check Medication Label**: Read label carefully three times
3. **Verify Dosage**: Confirm correct amount
4. **Check Expiration Date**: Ensure medication is not expired
5. **Review Allergies**: Check for any known allergies

### Post-Administration Monitoring
- Observe resident taking medication
- Ensure medication is swallowed, not pocketed
- Watch for immediate adverse reactions
- Document time and confirmation

## Special Considerations

### For Residents with Swallowing Difficulties
- Check if medication can be crushed (some cannot)
- Offer applesauce or pudding for easier swallowing
- Use liquid alternatives when available
- Report persistent difficulties to healthcare provider

### For Residents with Memory Issues
- Use visual aids and written schedules
- Create medication routine with meals
- Implement pill organizers with clear labels
- Double-check that medications weren't already taken

### For Diabetic Residents
- Always check blood glucose levels before insulin
- Keep glucose tablets or juice nearby
- Know signs of hypoglycemia (low blood sugar)
- Never skip diabetes medications

## Medication Side Effects to Monitor

### Common Side Effects
- Dizziness or lightheadedness
- Nausea or upset stomach
- Drowsiness or fatigue
- Dry mouth or constipation

### Serious Side Effects (Immediate Medical Attention)
- Difficulty breathing or swallowing
- Severe rash or hives
- Chest pain or irregular heartbeat
- Severe confusion or disorientation
- Uncontrolled bleeding

## Medication Interactions

### Foods to Avoid
- **Grapefruit**: Interacts with many blood pressure and cholesterol medications
- **Alcohol**: Can interfere with pain medications and antibiotics
- **Caffeine**: May interact with heart medications
- **High-Vitamin K Foods**: Can affect blood thinners

### Common Drug Interactions
- Blood thinners + Pain medications (bleeding risk)
- Diabetes medication + Steroids (blood sugar issues)
- Blood pressure medication + Decongestants (elevated BP)

## Missed Dose Protocol

### If a Dose is Missed
1. **Within 1 Hour**: Generally safe to take immediately
2. **1-2 Hours Late**: Contact pharmacist or healthcare provider
3. **More Than 2 Hours**: Skip dose, take next as scheduled
4. **Never Double Dose**: Unless specifically instructed by doctor

### Documentation Requirements
- Record missed dose time
- Document reason if known
- Note any follow-up actions taken
- Report to healthcare provider

## Emergency Procedures

### Medication Overdose
1. Call 911 immediately
2. Have medication bottle ready for emergency responders
3. Note time and amount taken
4. Monitor vital signs
5. Do NOT induce vomiting unless instructed

### Allergic Reaction
1. Stop medication immediately
2. Call for medical help
3. Administer EpiPen if prescribed and trained
4. Document reaction details
5. Update allergy list immediately

## Storage and Organization

### Proper Storage
- Store in cool, dry place away from sunlight
- Keep medications in original containers
- Separate morning, afternoon, and evening medications
- Store controlled substances securely

### Organization Tips
- Use weekly pill organizers
- Color-code medication times
- Keep emergency medications easily accessible
- Maintain current medication list

## Communication with Healthcare Providers

### Regular Updates
- Report any new side effects
- Notify of missed doses
- Discuss medication effectiveness
- Ask about potential interactions

### Information to Provide
- Complete medication list (including supplements)
- Timing of doses
- Any difficulties with compliance
- Questions or concerns

## Compliance Monitoring

### Tracking Methods
- Digital medication logs
- Photo documentation
- Daily checklists
- Weekly reviews with family

### Non-Compliance Red Flags
- Frequent missed doses
- Medications found hidden
- Reluctance to take medications
- Confusion about medication purpose

## Best Practices for AI Companions

### Communication Style
- Use calm, friendly tone
- Provide positive reinforcement
- Explain medication purposes simply
- Respect resident privacy and dignity

### Adaptability
- Adjust reminder timing to resident preferences
- Learn individual routines and habits
- Be flexible with notification methods
- Personalize interactions

### Safety First
- Always prioritize resident safety
- When in doubt, consult healthcare provider
- Document everything thoroughly
- Escalate concerns promptly
