# Activity and Engagement Guidelines

## Physical Activities

### Morning Exercise Routine (8:30 AM - 9:00 AM)
- Gentle stretching exercises
- Chair yoga for 15 minutes
- Balance exercises with supervision
- Walking in garden or corridor
- Range of motion activities

Benefits:
- Improves circulation
- Maintains flexibility
- Reduces fall risk
- Enhances mood
- Promotes better sleep

### Afternoon Activities (2:00 PM - 3:30 PM)
- Group games (bingo, cards, board games)
- Arts and crafts
- Music therapy sessions
- Gardening activities
- Pet therapy visits

## Cognitive Engagement

### Memory Activities
- Photo albums and life story sharing
- Word puzzles and crosswords
- Memory matching games
- Current events discussions
- Reminiscence therapy

### Learning Opportunities
- Educational videos on topics of interest
- Book clubs and reading groups
- Technology learning sessions
- Cultural events and presentations
- Guest speaker programs

## Social Engagement

### Group Activities
- Morning coffee social (10:00 AM)
- Afternoon tea time (3:00 PM)
- Weekly movie nights (Friday 7:00 PM)
- Sunday family gathering (2:00 PM)
- Monthly birthday celebrations

### One-on-One Interactions
- Daily conversation time
- Personal interest activities
- Letter writing or video calls with family
- Individual hobby time
- Spiritual care visits

## Entertainment Options

### Music and Performance
- Live musical performances
- Sing-alongs and karaoke
- Dance therapy sessions
- Instrument playing opportunities
- Classical music appreciation

### Arts and Culture
- Art appreciation sessions
- Painting and drawing classes
- Craft projects
- Museum virtual tours
- Cultural heritage celebrations

## Outdoor Activities

### Garden and Nature
- Therapeutic gardening
- Bird watching
- Nature walks
- Outdoor picnics
- Sensory garden visits

### Weather Considerations
- Summer: Early morning or late afternoon activities
- Winter: Indoor alternatives, warm clothing for outdoor time
- Rainy days: Covered patio activities
- Always check temperature and air quality

## Rest and Relaxation

### Quiet Time Activities
- Reading in library
- Listening to audiobooks
- Guided meditation
- Gentle music listening
- Aromatherapy sessions

### Sleep Promotion
- Limit caffeine after 2 PM
- Encourage afternoon rest period (1:00 PM - 2:00 PM)
- Maintain consistent bedtime routine
- Reduce stimulation in evening
- Ensure comfortable room temperature

## Holiday and Special Events

### Monthly Themes
- January: New Year celebrations
- February: Valentine's Day crafts
- March: Spring activities
- April: Easter egg decorating
- And continuing through the year...

### Special Celebrations
- Birthday parties with cake and singing
- Anniversary recognitions
- Religious holiday observances
- Seasonal festivals
- Achievement awards

## Activity Adaptation

### For Limited Mobility
- Seated exercises
- Hand and finger activities
- Visual engagement (videos, photos)
- Audio entertainment
- Adapted games and crafts

### For Cognitive Challenges
- Simplified instructions
- Familiar activities from past
- Sensory stimulation
- Music from their era
- One-step tasks

### For Sensory Impairments
- Large print materials
- Amplified audio systems
- Tactile activities
- Visual cues and demonstrations
- Well-lit environments

## Resident Choice and Autonomy

### Respecting Preferences
- Offer activity choices daily
- Allow opt-out without pressure
- Accommodate personal schedules
- Honor cultural preferences
- Support individual interests

### Encouraging Participation
- Use positive encouragement
- Start with familiar activities
- Pair with friends or buddies
- Provide appropriate assistance
- Celebrate small successes

## Documentation and Assessment

### Activity Participation Logs
Track for each resident:
- Activities attended
- Duration of participation
- Level of engagement
- Social interactions
- Mood and behavior

### Quarterly Assessment
Review and update:
- Activity preferences
- Functional abilities
- Social engagement levels
- Cognitive status
- Physical capabilities

## Safety During Activities

### General Safety Guidelines
- Always supervise group activities
- Ensure proper lighting
- Remove tripping hazards
- Have emergency equipment nearby
- Know each resident's limitations

### Activity-Specific Safety
- Water activities: Monitor hydration, bathroom access
- Physical activities: Check vital signs, watch for fatigue
- Craft activities: Use safe materials, supervise sharp tools
- Outdoor activities: Apply sunscreen, provide shade, hydration

## Family Involvement

### Encouraging Family Participation
- Invite to special events
- Schedule regular visit times
- Provide activity calendars
- Suggest activities families can do
- Share photos and videos

### Volunteer Programs
- Reading companions
- Music performers
- Art instructors
- Gardening helpers
- Pet therapy handlers
